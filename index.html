Mission

There are kids today in this country who go without student lunches. Our mission is to bring that number down to zero. We provide an efficient method for schools to broadcast the needs of their students by declaring a monetary goal that they need to reach in order to provide lunches for any students that may not have the necessary means to obtain one.

Values

We believe every child has the right to a nutritious lunch. Food is not only vital to a child's learning capacity but lunchtime plays a vast role on a child's social interaction with other students. According to USDA, the new regulations added 10 cents to the cost of preparing every school lunch and 27 cents for every breakfast. To help schools meet the rules, Congress provided school meal programs only 6 additional cents for each lunch and no additional funds for breakfast.

A study found that, on average, the full cost to produce a reimbursable school lunch was $2.91, exceeding the free lunch subsidy, which at the time of the study was conducted, was $2.51. To boost operational revenue, many school meal programs rely on a la carte sales, provide catering services or contract with community programs

Children from families with incomes at or below 130% of the poverty level are eligible for free school meals. Those with incomes between 130% and 185% of the poverty level are eligible for reduced price meals (student pays 30 cents for breakfast and 40 cents for lunch). For the 2018-19 school year, 130% of the poverty level is $32,630 for a family of four and 185% is $46,435.
